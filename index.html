May Trading Behavior Analysis
Signal patterns & market context
ZEC — Exploiting Volatility (+20.3%)
Hackworth Prime on ZEC executed frequent BUY/SELL rotations throughout May, capitalizing on ZEC's elevated intraday volatility. The strategy's 27.15% average net exposure allowed it to remain nimble, taking profits quickly and re-entering on reversals. With an average winning trade of 3.83% versus average losses of -2.21%, the asymmetric payoff ratio was key to the outsized monthly return. The ROI curve shows consistent upward trajectory with controlled pullbacks, indicating effective drawdown management even during the most volatile sessions.
XMR — Both Variants Win (Prime +6.7%, OptimaShort +8.8%)
A fascinating case study: OptimaShort outperformed Prime on XMR by 220 bps in May. XMR traded in a choppy range with several false breakouts, conditions that favored OptimaShort's increased short-bias and mean-reversion weighting. The strategy actively added short positions at local highs and long positions at support, with trade logs showing rapid cycle times between entries and exits. Prime's lower 17.54% net exposure meant it captured less of the volatility, but with lower stress on the portfolio.
BNB — Range-Bound Efficiency (Prime +8.3%, OptimaShort +5.8%)
BNB's relatively contained price action in May suited Prime's balanced approach better than OptimaShort's defensive posture. Prime captured both upside and downside moves with ~32% average net exposure, while OptimaShort's heavier short allocation missed some of the bullish reversals within the range. OptimaShort BNB still delivered an impressive Sharpe ratio of 3.52, the highest across all six strategies, reflecting its ultra-smooth equity curve.
LINK — Steady Accumulation (+3.1%)
LINK exhibited lower volatility in May compared to other assets, resulting in more modest but highly consistent returns for OptimaShort. The strategy executed 4,278+ trades historically, demonstrating high-frequency alpha generation. With the lowest max drawdown of 22.42% and a strong Sharpe of 2.60, OptimaShort LINK represents the most defensive allocation in the Hackworth lineup, ideal for capital preservation with steady growth.
