ZEC — Exploiting Volatility (+20.3%)
Hackworth Prime on ZEC executed approximately 210 BUY/SELL rotations throughout May, capitalizing on ZEC's elevated intraday volatility. The strategy's 27.15% average net exposure allowed it to remain nimble, taking profits quickly and re-entering on reversals. With an average winning trade of 3.83% versus average losses of -2.21%, the asymmetric payoff ratio was key to the outsized monthly return. The ROI curve shows consistent upward trajectory with controlled pullbacks, indicating effective drawdown management even during the most volatile sessions.
XMR — Both Variants Win (Prime +6.7%, OptimaShort +8.8%)
A fascinating case study from the selected preview: OptimaShort outperformed Prime on XMR by 220 bps in May. XMR traded in a choppy range with several false breakouts, conditions that favored OptimaShort's increased short-bias and mean-reversion weighting. The strategy actively added short positions at local highs and long positions at support, with trade logs showing rapid cycle times between entries and exits. Prime's lower 17.54% net exposure meant it captured less of the volatility, but with lower stress on the portfolio.
BNB — Range-Bound Efficiency (Prime +8.3%, OptimaShort +5.8%)
BNB's relatively contained price action in May suited Prime's balanced multi-alpha approach better than OptimaShort's defensive posture. Prime captured both upside and downside moves with ~32% average net exposure, while OptimaShort's heavier short allocation missed some of the bullish reversals within the range. OptimaShort BNB still delivered an impressive Sharpe ratio of 3.52, the highest across all six selected strategies, reflecting its ultra-smooth equity curve.
LINK — Steady Accumulation (+3.1%)
LINK exhibited lower volatility in May compared to other assets in the selection, resulting in more modest but highly consistent returns for OptimaShort. The strategy executed ~140 trades during the month, demonstrating disciplined, high-frequency alpha generation. With the lowest max drawdown of 22.42% and a strong Sharpe of 2.60, OptimaShort LINK represents the most defensive allocation in this preview set, ideal for capital preservation with steady growth.

Hackworth Series Engine — Technical Architecture
SOTA Deep Learning Pipeline
The Engine Behind the Numbers
State-of-the-art multi-alpha quantitative AI system
The Hackworth Series is AlphaNet's flagship multi-alpha strategy engine, built on a proprietary deep learning framework. It is not a single model but an ensemble orchestration layer that combines up to 20 distinct alpha signals, each generated by its own specialized deep learning model, into one unified hedged portfolio. The engine serves as both a deployed strategy series and a continuous alpha discovery factory.
🧠 Model-Agnostic Deep Learning Ensemble
Hackworth does not rely on a single architecture. Instead, it runs a heterogeneous ensemble of deep learning model families, each optimized for different market microstructure patterns. The system dynamically weights contributions from:
TF
Transformers
LS
LSTM Networks
GR
GRU Models
CN
CNN Extractors
Transformer models excel at capturing long-range dependencies and global market regime context. LSTM and GRU variants specialize in sequential pattern recognition for momentum and trend persistence. CNN-based models perform local feature extraction from price-volume surfaces, identifying microstructural patterns such as support/resistance clustering and order book imbalance artifacts. The model router allocates compute dynamically based on prevailing market entropy.
💾 Data Pipeline Architecture
The ingestion pipeline processes multi-modal market data in real-time:
Price & Volume (L1/L2 OHLCV)
Order Book Dynamics (bid/ask depth, imbalance)
Funding Rate & Open Interest (perp derivatives)
Cross-Market Correlation Matrix (10+ pairs)
On-Chain Flow Metrics (wallet concentration, exchange flows)
Volatility Surface (realized & implied term structure)
All features are normalized through adaptive z-scoring with rolling lookback windows that auto-adjust based on regime volatility. The pipeline handles ~50,000 data points per minute per trading pair.
🔄 Parameter Search Space
Each alpha strategy within the Hackworth framework is the result of exhaustive hyperparameter search across:
Model Architecture: 12 variants
Lookback Windows: 15min to 30 days
Feature Subsets: 256 combinations
Position Sizing Rules: 8 risk models
Entry/Exit Thresholds: 64 levels
Ensemble Weight Schemes: 16 methods
This yields ~50 million+ permutations per alpha candidate. A proprietary genetic algorithm with early stopping prunes 99.7% of configurations before full backtesting, making the search computationally tractable.
⚡ Dynamic Trading Rules Mechanism
Unlike static rule-based strategies, Hackworth employs a meta-learning controller that adjusts trading rules in real-time:
  • Regime Detection: A hidden Markov model classifies market state into 6 regimes (trend-up, trend-down, range-bound, volatile breakout, low-volatility accumulation, correlation breakdown)
  • Alpha Selector: Based on regime classification, the controller reweights the 20 constituent alphas. Trend-following alphas receive higher allocation in directional regimes; mean-reversion alphas dominate in range-bound conditions
  • Kelly-Optimal Sizing: Position sizes are computed via a fractional Kelly criterion modified with drawdown circuit breakers. Maximum single-trade risk is capped at 2% of portfolio value
  • Correlation Hedge: When inter-alpha correlation spikes above 0.4, the engine automatically reduces exposure to prevent cluster risk
🌀 Multi-Alpha Composition
The term "multi-alpha" refers to the strategy's construction from up to 20 distinct, independently trained deep learning strategies (alphas), each producing its own trading signals:
Alpha 1-5: Momentum/Trend Following (LSTM/GRU based)
Alpha 6-10: Mean Reversion (CNN + Transformer hybrid)
Alpha 11-14: Volatility Expansion (autoencoder anomaly det.)
Alpha 15-17: Cross-Market Arbitrage (correlation breakdown)
Alpha 18-20: Microstructure (order book, flow prediction)
Each alpha is a complete, standalone deep learning strategy with its own data pipeline, model architecture, and signal generator. They are trained on different time horizons, feature sets, and objective functions to ensure low cross-correlation (target <0.15 pairwise). The ensemble layer combines these 20 signals using a learned attention mechanism that adapts to which alpha types are performing best in the current regime. This is the core innovation that produces the Hackworth series' superior risk-adjusted returns: when one alpha class underperforms, others typically compensate, producing a smoother equity curve than any single-strategy approach could achieve.
📡 Real-Time Online Training
Models are not trained once and deployed. Instead, Hackworth uses continual learning with Elastic Weight Consolidation (EWC) to update model parameters every 4 hours on the latest market data. This prevents catastrophic forgetting while allowing the system to adapt to evolving market microstructure. All online updates are validated through a shadow sandbox before being promoted to production trading.
🛡 Risk Control Layer
Beyond position sizing, a dedicated risk neural network monitors portfolio heat in real-time. It enforces hard limits on: gross exposure (max 150%), net exposure (variant-dependent: 17-32%), drawdown circuit breakers (reduce at -5% daily, halt at -10%), and VaR-based dynamic leverage scaling. This multi-layered safety system is why no selected strategy approached its historical max drawdown during May.
Three Engine Variants
Hackworth Prime
The balanced flagship. Low ~25% directional exposure, relatively balanced across all 20 alphas. Designed to weather all market conditions while capturing fat-tail moves. Best for general deployment.
Hackworth Trend
Long-bias, fat-tail heavy version. Maximizes returns in uptrends while maintaining reduced short exposure. Available for deployment during strongly directional regimes.
Hackworth OptimaShort
Mean-reversion and short-bias heavy. The most risk-averse variant delivering lower but steadier returns with smaller drawdowns. Thrives in choppy, range-bound conditions.
